Hi, I’m Joshua!
I’m a (student) journalist and entrepreneur interested in telling impactful stories via experimental and emerging platforms.
Here’s some of my writing, and here’s a piece of writing I’m particularly fond of. Currently, I’m an occasional contributor to The Yappie, a news publication covering AAPI politics and issues.

In the past, I’ve worked on the New Journalism Collective, an Instagram-based reporting platform staffed by student journalists representing 12 states. I’m also a proud AAJA member.

Although I’ll always be a Bay Area native at heart, I’m attending Princeton University starting fall 2021. Let’s chat on Twitter, LinkedIn or over email!
I also code a bit — enough to add animations into my website but not enough to make them run smoothly. Here are some of my favorite books; I ran a lit journal for a while and published many lovely poems. I also made an attempt at a TEDx talk about student journalism once.
My favorite books, 2021 [hide]

“I believe that the books and stories we fall in love with make us who we are, or, not to claim too much, the beloved tale becomes a part of the way in which we understand things and make judgments and choices in our daily lives.” (Salman Rushdie)

On Earth We’re Briefly Gorgeous, by Ocean Vuong: “Because the sunset, like survival, exists only on the verge of its own disappearing. To be gorgeous, you must first be seen, but to be seen allows you to be hunted.”

I’ll Give You The Sun, by Jandy Nelson: “We were all heading for each other on a collision course, no matter what. Maybe some people are just meant to be in the same story.”

A Farewell to Arms, by Ernest Hemingway: “After a while I went out and left the hospital and walked back to the hotel in the rain.”

When Breath Becomes Air, by Paul Kalanithi: “And with that, the future I had imagined, the one just about to be realized, the culmination of decades of striving, evaporated.”

Spacestruck, by Paige Lewis: “I feel / as if I’m on the moon listening to the air hiss / out of my spacesuit, and I can’t find the hole. I’m / the vice president of panic, and the president is / missing.”

The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle, by Haruki Murakami: “I thought of May Kasahara, with her blue wool hat, along on the bus taking her back to her factory in the hills. Then I thought of the duck people, asleep in the grassy shadows somewhere. And finally, I thought of the world that I was heading back to.”

Night Sky With Exit Wounds, by Ocean Vuong: “At home, I threw myself on the bed like a torch & watched the flames / gnaw through my mother’s house until the sky appeared, bloodshot & massive. / How I wanted to be that sky—to hold every flight & fall at once.”
