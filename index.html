I’m a student and freelance journalist with a focus reporting on migration, diaspora, and challenges to democracy around the globe. I’ve covered everything from Indian migrants in Israel and the Hong Kong pro-democracy movement to Chinese social media and Armenians in Jerusalem.

My writing appears in Foreign Policy, The Nation, Rest of World, LA Review of Books, and elsewhere. I also do some editing work; I currently edit nonfiction reviews and essays for The Oxonian Review, and I previously edited reviews, interviews, and self-essays for The Daily Princetonian.

Journalism aside, I’m a third-year student at Princeton University, where I study philosophy; for the first half of 2024, I’m at Oxford University as a visiting student. I was born and raised in California, but I’ve spent enough time in New York, Hong Kong, and Paris that I could tell you where my favorite bookstores are in each city.

To contact me, DM me on Twitter or shoot me an email.

Background: I’m drawn to media-adjacent startups for the breadth and depth of skills required, from planning editorial direction to managing staffers to very often having to bootstrap the software (websites, etc) behind everything.

Past publications I’ve launched include the New Journalism Collective (5000+ impressions on Instagram) and Anser Journal (~10K+ monthly Twitter impressions). I also have traditional experience in writing/reporting; most recently, I’ve been a writer for The Yappie, a publication focused on elevating coverage of AAPI power and politics. Writing awards include publication in Scholastic Inc.’s The Best Teen Writing of 2020 and an Al Neuharth Free Spirit scholarship from the Freedom Forum.

Some things I’ve been involved with recently:
  • Navigating college @ Princeton and taking classes in philosophy, journalism, and CS.
  • I recently wrapped up a summer spent doing growth, marketing, and biz ops work at Zette, a startup that embodies the type of Silicon Valley x journalism collaboration I’m enthusiastic about.
  • Living in Paris on a budget. [Note: Back in the US now, somewhat unfortunately]
  • Connecting with people working in media, tech, VC, or similar spaces for insightful conversations!
Long-term: I’m increasingly concerned about the journalism industry’s struggle to adapt to the modern media ecosystem, especially as social media siphons readers away from newspapers. Silicon Valley and journalism are often at odds with each other, but I think there’s a clear intersection between the two spaces — to uphold the mission/ethics of journalism while pulling in readers and being financially sustainable (see: Substack, Medium, Column, etc). In the future, I’d love to be working in that intersection via my own startup or as part of an early-stage startup, but I’m open to other possibilities that come my way!

我的文章主要探討都市、移民和僑民社區，特別側重於通過現實生活的實例來進行專題報道。我今年夏天在從香港寫文章，由於普林斯頓大學 Dale 獎學金的支持。我為《The Yappie》（一家探索亞裔美國政治的新聞媒體）供稿，也是《The Daily Princetonian》（普林斯頓大學生主要媒體）的編輯和專欄作者。在這裡可以閱讀我的文章。

寫作以外, 我對新聞業如何應用新科技有特別的興趣。去年夏天我在Zette（一家媒體創業公司）實習，幫助它改進媒體內容的收費訂閱方式，並擴大優質媒體內容的獲得渠道，包括規劃、公關、營銷和業務運營，在這裡可以了解我的工作。

我現在是一位普林斯頓大學哲學系的二年級學生，對倫理、元倫理和未來倫理感興趣。我也編程和寫作。
