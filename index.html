Background: I’m drawn to media-adjacent startups for the breadth and depth of skills required, from planning editorial direction to managing staffers to very often having to bootstrap the software (websites, etc) behind everything.

Past publications I’ve launched include the New Journalism Collective (5000+ impressions on Instagram) and Anser Journal (~10K+ monthly Twitter impressions). I also have traditional experience in writing/reporting; most recently, I’ve been a writer for The Yappie, a publication focused on elevating coverage of AAPI power and politics. Writing awards include publication in Scholastic Inc.’s The Best Teen Writing of 2020 and an Al Neuharth Free Spirit scholarship from the Freedom Forum.

Long-term: I’m increasingly concerned about the journalism industry’s struggle to adapt to the modern media ecosystem, especially as social media siphons readers away from newspapers. Silicon Valley and journalism are often at odds with each other, but I think there’s a clear intersection between the two spaces — to uphold the mission/ethics of journalism while pulling in readers and being financially sustainable (see: Substack, Medium, Column, etc). In the future, I’d love to be working in that intersection via my own startup or as part of an early-stage startup, but I’m open to other possibilities that come my way!
